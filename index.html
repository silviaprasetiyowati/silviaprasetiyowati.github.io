// Thinking

Research & writing.

Master's Thesis · CBS · 2024

Personalisation, TikTok & Digital Consumer Shaping

How TikTok's algorithm shapes Gen Alpha's relationship with advertising — and what it means for sustainability brands trying to drive real behavior change.

Qualitative research with 10 Gen Alpha participants in Denmark — combining consumer psychology, behavioral science, and qualitative methodology. The findings challenge how sustainability brands approach digital communication: moving beyond awareness campaigns toward genuine information literacy and behavior change.

Finding 01

Brand and ad relevance relies heavily on ad-attractiveness — not just message content or sustainability claims.

Finding 02

Offline interaction remains essential for meaningful online engagement and genuine trust-building.

Finding 03

Internal motivation is an underexplored but promising factor in personalisation — with real implications for behavior change campaigns.

LinkedIn Article · 2025

Can We Really Make Consumers More Conscious of Sustainable Choices?

The gap between sustainability awareness and actual behavior change — and what brands can do differently.

Read →

Drawing from behavioral economics, cognitive psychology, and field observations at Deep Green Innovators and Copenhagen sustainability events. Argues that brands need to move from information campaigns to designing environments that make sustainable choices the default.
